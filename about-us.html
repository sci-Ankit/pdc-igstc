About The PDC-IT 2023

Precursor Derived Ceramics-Innovations & Translational Research (PDC-IT) is committed to setting up a forum for discussing cutting-edge research methodologies and exchanging the most recent research findings in precursor derived ceramics. Over the course of two days, eminent speakers from Germany and India will discuss about the enormous diversified applications that precursor derived ceramics bestows especially in line with the demands of energy, environment, and health care sectors. These lectures will be sandwiched between talks by the young researchers.

Additionally, keeping in mind the translational necessity in a predominantly science-driven field, talks from start-ups and various national and international industrial partners will be presented. To also appreciate the benefits of applying computational thermodynamics to the real-world complex scenario of polymer derived ceramics, special talks are being organized. PDC-IT will be a significant and valuable forum for encouraging cross-cultural and interdisciplinary dialogue in the fields of molecular processing, advanced polymer precursors, functional ceramics and provides a gateway to encourage translational research and entrepreneurship.

Venue

The conference will be held at the Centre for Industrial Consultancy and Sponsored Research (ICSR) auditorium, Indian Institute of Technology Madras, Chennai. IIT Madras is one among the foremost institutes of national importance in higher technological education, basic and applied research. It has been the top-ranking engineering institute in India for the past 7 years in the National Institutional Ranking Framework (NIRF), Government of India rankings since this ranking system was announced in 2015. Much of the campus is a protected forest, carved out of the Guindy National Park, home to many animals that are not commonly seen in a busy city like Chennai.

Location: The campus is conveniently located about 10 km from Chennai airport and 12 km from Chennai central railway station and is well connected by city buses, sub-urban and metro train services. Kasturba Nagar is the nearest station on the Chennai MRTS line and Little mount is the nearest station in the Chennai Metro line. Auto and taxi services are also easily accessible to the campus.
